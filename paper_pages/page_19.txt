Figure 2. 
Concept of tissue strain mapping (TSM) for detecting strain-induced stiffness changes 
in tissues adjacent to a rapidly expanding lesion. (A) Rapid lesion growth stretches the 
surrounding perilesional tissue. (B) Transmission of a planar shear wave through the lesion-
host tissue allows for the detection of increased circumferential shear stiffness in regions 
stretched parallel to the wave propagation direction. (C) Rotation of the wave propagation 
vector along the lesion-host boundary alters the apparent local shear stiffness. Combining 
shear stiffness estimates across all propagation directions could reveal local strain effects 
around the expanding lesion.
